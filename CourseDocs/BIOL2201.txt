EVALUATION:

This is an introductory level course designed to prepare the students for courses in marine environmental technology.

Introduction to Microbiology; Microscopy and Staining; Prokaryotic Microorganisms; Eukaryotic Microorganisms; Viruses; Microbial Growth; Control of Microbial Growth; Marine Environment and Microorganisms; Microbial Ecology of the Oceans; Marine Microbes and Human Society; Drinking Water and Wastewater Treatment

BIOL 1100 (Biology)

Duration:	13 weeks
Lectures:	3 hours/week = 39 hours total Laboratory:	3 hours/week = 39 hours total

1)  To enable students to identify the nature, characteristics, and importance of marine microorganisms.

2)  To enable students to identify the diversity of marine microbial life.

3)  To enable the student to understand how marine microbes impact on human activities.

4)  To develop student’s awareness and competency in basic microbiological techniques and applications.

LEARNING OBJECTIVES:
THE EXPECTED LEARNING OUTCOME IS THAT THE STUDENT WILL BE ABLE TO:

1.0  Introduction to Microbiology
1.1  Overview
* Define “microorganism” and “microbiology”.
* List the important properties of microorganisms.
* List the major organism groups.
1.2  Importance of Microorganisms
* List at least five beneficial activities of microorganisms.
* List three harmful activities of microorganisms.
1.3  Prokaryotic and Eukaryotic Microorganisms
* Distinguish between eukaryotes and prokaryotes.
1.4  Nomenclature
* Define “binomial nomenclature”.
* Discuss the purpose of binomial nomenclature.
* Provide the rationale for the system of scientific names.
1.5  History
* Discuss some major events in the history of microbiology

2.0  Microscopy and Staining
2.1  Microscopes
* List the major functions of a microscope.
* Define “resolution” and “magnification”.
* Describe the different parts and functions of a compound microscope.
* Diagram the path of light through a compound microscope.
* Understand the use and characteristics of phase-contrast, dark-field, fluorescence, and electron microscopes.
2.2  Staining Techniques
* Discuss the importance of staining microorganisms.
* Compare simple differential and special staining techniques.
* Perform gram staining, acid-staining, and endospore staining on given specimens.

3.0  Prokaryotic Microorganisms
3.1  Shape, Arrangement, and Size
* List the units of measurements used for microorganisms.
* Identify the shapes and arrangements of bacteria.
* State the sizes of bacteria.
3.2  Bacterial Structures
* Describe the structure, chemistry, and functions of flagella, pili ,glycocalyx, cell wall and cell membrane.
* Describe the structure, chemistry, and functions of cytoplasm, mesosomes, DNA, and cytoplasmic granules.
3.3  Endospores
* List the bacterial genera-producing spores.
* Discuss structure and chemistry of spores.

4.0  Eukaryotic Microorganisms
4.1  Fungi
* Identify the microorganisms which belong to the kingdom of fungi.

* Discuss the general properties, method of reproduction and importance of fungi.

* List the defining characteristics of four phyla of fungi.

* Name the media used for the cultivation of molds.
* Identify important marine molds.
4.5  Protozoa
* Describe the morphology , and method of reproduction of protozoa.
* List the defining characteristics of the four phyla of protozoa.
* Classify protozoa.
* Identify important marine protozoa.
* Identify the protozoa in a given sample.
4.6  Algae
* Describe the general characteristics of algae.
* Describe the structure and method of reproduction of algae.
* Identify the algae in a given sample.
* Identify important marine algae.

5.0  Viruses
5.1  Characteristics
* Describe the general properties, host range, and importance of viruses.
5.2  Structure
* Illustrate the structure of a virus particle.
* List functions of nucleic acid, capsid, capsomere, and spikes.
5.3  Isolation and Cultivation
* Discuss three methods of viruses cultivation.
5.4  Multiplication
* Discuss the multiplication cycle of viruses.
5.5  Effects of Viral Infection on Host Cell
* List the effects of animal viral infection on host cells.
5.6  Bacteriophages
* Define “bacteriophages”.
* Identify the structures of bacteriophages.
* Discuss the multiplication of bacteriophages.
* Discus the effects of bacteriophage infection on the host cell.

6.0  Microbial Growth
6.1  Bacterial Reproduction
* Define “bacterial growth”.
* Describe how bacteria multiply by binary fission.
* Define “generation time”.
* Discuss the importance of generation time.
6.2  Requirements for Growth
* List the nutritional factors required for growth and discuss the importance of each factor.
* List the physical factors required for growth.
* Discuss the importance of each physical factor required for growth.
* Explain how microorganisms are classified on the basis of oxygen requirement and temperature requirement.
* 	Suggest experiments to determine the optimum growth temperature and oxygen requirement of the given bacteria.
6.3  Growth Phases
* Compare four different phases of growth and their relation to generation time.
6.4  Cultivation Techniques
* Define “medium”.
* List the different forms and types of culture media and their uses.
* Justify the use of selective and differential media.
* Prepare media for the cultivation of microorganisms.
* Cultivate a given bacteria using aseptic technique.
* Use a pour plate and a spread plate method to cultivate bacteria.
* Cultivate an anaerobic bacteria.
* Describe methods to isolate pure culture.
* Isolate a pure culture of bacteria from a given specimen.
6.5  Preservation of Cultures
* Describe methods of culture preservation.
6.6  Measurement of Growth
* List methods for determining microbial growth and describe the important features of each method.

7.0  Control of Microbial Growth
7.1  Overview
* Define terms related to the destruction and suppression of microbial growth.
* Discuss the factors that influence the control of microbial growth.
7.2  Physical Methods of Control
* Describe the physical methods of microbial growth control and their standard uses.
* Sterilize the given samples/objects using an appropriate method.
* Use the steam sterilization apparatus efficiently.
7.3  Chemical Agents
* Describe the modes of action and the preferred uses of disinfectants.
* Use an appropriate disinfectant to disinfect a given object.

7.4  Chemotherapeutic Agents
* List two types of chemotherapeutic agents.
* Define “antibiotic”.
* Discuss the mode of action of different agents and their antimicrobial activities.
* Describe the non-medical uses of antibiotics.

8.0  Marine Environment and Microorganisms
* Identify the ecological zones of the oceans and marine microbial habitats.
* Identify the microorganisms in deep-sea trenches and hypothermal vents.
* Describe the chemical and physical factors in the marine environment and discuss the impact of these factors on marine microbes.

9.0  Microbial Ecology of the Oceans
9.1  Symbiotic Associations
* Define “Symbiosis”.
* Discuss different types of symbiotic relationships.
* Discuss the symbiotic association of microalgae with animals.
* Discuss the symbiotic association of prokaryotes with animals.
* Discuss the light symbioses in fish and invertebrates.
* Identify microbial symbionts of sponges and their activity.
9.2  Biogeochemical Roles of Marine Microbes
* Discuss the role of marine microbial activities in carbon cycle.
* Discuss the role of marine microbial activities in nitrogen cycle.
* Discuss the role of marine microbial activities in sulfur cycle.
9.3  Marine Pathogens
* Outline the steps of disease production by microorganisms.
* Distinguish between intoxication and infection.
* Name the diseases produced by marine pathogens in humans.
* Describe the mode of transmission, symptoms and control of microbial diseases of humans.
* Identify shellfish poisoning.
* Describe the mode of transmission, symptoms and control of shellfish poisoning.
* List the diseases produced by marine pathogens in mammals.
* Describe the mode of transmission, symptoms and control of microbial diseases of marine mammals.
* List the diseases produced by marine pathogens in fish.
* Describe the mode of transmission, symptoms and control of microbial diseases of marine fish.
* - Name the diseases produced by invertebrate pathogens.
* - Describe the mode of transmission, symptoms of the disease and control of microbial disease of marine invertebrates.
