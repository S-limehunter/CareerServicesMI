AQUA 4100
(Aquaculture Seminar Series)

TYPE AND PURPOSE:	This course will present selected current topics of relevance to the
development of the aquaculture industry.

CALENDAR ENTRY:	Newfoundland and Labrador Aquaculture Historical Development:
Current Topics in Finfish Aquaculture; Current Topics in Shellfish Aquaculture; Aquaculture Mandates of Provincial and Federal Agencies; Aquaculture Research; Aquaculture Associations
SCHEDULE:	Duration:	13 weeks
Lecture:	1.5 hours per week

COURSE AIMS:	1)	To explore the historical development in aquaculture in
Newfoundland and Labrador and other areas of the world.

2)  To assess the role played by government agencies and departments in the development of aquaculture in Newfoundland and Labrador.

3)  To introduce students to individuals, local companies and institutions involved in aquaculture research activity.

4)  To examine the role aquaculture associations play in industry development.



EVALUATION:

* This seminar course is graded PASS or FAIL based on attendance and participation.
* In order to achieve a PASS mark, students must attend a minimum of 10 classes and submit a one-page summary of the topic of each class attended.
