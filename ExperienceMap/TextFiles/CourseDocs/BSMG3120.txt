EVALUATION:

This course is designed to provide the knowledge and skills necessary to conduct the development of a new product and focuses on the steps needed to develop, process and package a food product.

Introduction to Marketing Concepts; Product Planning; The Conceptual Process of Product Development; Product Mix; Product Development for the Food Industry; Phases in Product Development; Food Science and Technology; Packaging in the Food Industry

Duration:	13 weeks
Lectures:	3 hours per week = 39 hours To enable students to:
1)  Have an opportunity to explore general marketing concepts and apply them to the food industry.

2)  Gain insight into product development as a process.

3)  Examine a systems approach developing to New Products.

4)  Enhance their ability to meet challenges and solve special problems in the food industry.

LEARNING OBJECTIVES:
THE EXPECTED LEARNING OUTCOME IS THAT THE STUDENT WILL BE ABLE TO:

1.0  Introduction to Marketing Concepts
1.1  The Field of Marketing
* Explain exchange as a marketing feature.
* Define "marketing" from a business perspective.
* Review the evolution of marketing.
* State the forms of utility.
1.2  Marketing Objectives
* Describe the steps in strategic market planning.
* Explain the "marketing mix."
* Review the components of a marketing plan.
* Survey planning models.
1.3  Market Segmentation
* Define "target marketing."
* Examine the concept of "segmentation."
* Discuss "positioning."

2.0  Product Planning
* State the marketing meaning of "product."
* Explain how products are classified.

3.0  The Conceptual Process of Product Development
* State the importance of product innovation.
* Differentiate categories of new products.
* List the steps in the development of new products.
* Catalogue the stages of new product adoption.
* Explain why new products succeed or fail.

4.0  Product Mix
* Review product positioning strategies.
* Explain the "Product Life Cycle."
* Name ways to suggest new product ideas.

5.0  Product Development for the Food Industry
5.1  New Food Product Development
* Define "new product."
* Explain the classes of new products.
* Discuss the concept of "value-added."
* Explain the five main reasons why food companies develop new products.
* List four reasons why industry may not pursue new products.
* Discuss the risks for new product development.
* List external and internal reasons why new products fail.
