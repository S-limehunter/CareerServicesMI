BIOL 4104
(Food Microbiology)

TYPE AND PURPOSE:	The purpose of this course is to introduce the student to the microbiology of foods. The important parameters controlling microbial growth in foods, the microorganisms involved in spoilage of foods and principles of microbial spoilage and their control will be discussed. Students will also be introduced to the methods of microbiological analysis of foods.

CALENDAR ENTRY:	Introduction; Characteristics of Microorganisms Associated with Foods; Sources of Microorganisms in Foods; Factors Influencing Microbial Growth in Foods; Food Spoilage; Food Preservation Methods; Microbial Analysis of Food and Food Environment; Beneficial uses of Microorganisms in Foods; Shelf-life and Challenge Studies

PREREQUISITES:	SC 1807 (Safety in Science Lab (Lab Safety)) and SC 1808 (WHMIS)

SCHEDULE:	Duration:	13 weeks
Lectures:	3 hours per week = 39 total hours Laboratories:	3 hours once per week = 39 total hours

COURSE AIMS:	To enable the student to develop knowledge of:
1)  The nature and properties of microorganisms important in foods.
2)  The sources of microorganisms in foods.
3)  The factors influencing microbial growth in foods.
4)  Spoilage and beneficial microorganisms in the food industry.
5)  The microbiological aspects of food preservation methods.
6)  And experience in basic and advanced microbiological techniques for isolating and quantifying spoilage and pathogenic microorganisms present in foods and the food processing environment.
7)  And experience in designing shelf-life and challenge studies.

* Distinguish between standard and recommended methods.

7.2  Sample and Sampling Plans

* Describe methods of sample selection, collection, preparation, storage and transport to the testing lab.
* Select a "representative sample" and use it for microbiological analysis.
* Distinguish between Mandatory criteria and advisory criteria.
* Distinguish between Microbiological standard and guideline.

* Multiple Attribute Plan
* Two Class Attribute Plan
* Three Class Attribute Plan

7.3  Quantitative Methods
7.3.1  Direct Enumeration

* Discuss the principle, outline the method and identify the advantages/disadvantages of the total plate count method.
* Determine the total or aerobic plate count in a given food sample.
* Discuss the principle, outline methods, identify uses and discuss advantages and disadvantages of filtration methods for determining bacterial counts in food or water samples.
* Determine bacterial count of a given sample using membrane filtration method.
7.3.2  Indirect Estimation
* Discuss the principle, outline the method and identify the use and advantages/disadvantages of the following methods:
* Most Probable Number (MPN) Method
* Dye Reduction Test
* Impedance Method
* Radiometry Method
* Estimate the microbial count in given water or food sample using MPN method.
* Estimate the microbial count in a given milk sample using resazurin and litmus blue dye reduction tests.
